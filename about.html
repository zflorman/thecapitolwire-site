The Capitol Wire

Automated legislative intelligence for the modern staffer.

Why is this free?

Because I'm not a K Street firm trying to sell you a $20,000 subscription. I'm a developer who used to work on the Hill. The data is public, the hosting is cheap, and I believe access to real-time legislative tracking shouldn't be a luxury good. No ads, no tracking, no catch.

Why I built it

I spent time as a staffer, so I know the drill: refreshing the Rules Committee website, waiting for the Clerk to post PDFs, and relying on rumor to know when a bill text changed.

It honestly blew my mind that with all this data published as XML, there wasn't already an automated system to parse it instantly. We shouldn't have to manually hunt for information that is digitally available. I got tired of waiting for a better tool, so I wrote the code myself.
