Democracy shouldn't have a paywall.

Why I built an AI to read Congress so you don't have to.

I used to be a staffer on the Hill. I remember the late nights, the cold coffee, and the sheer panic of staring at a 2,000-page bill that dropped at midnight, knowing we had to vote on it by morning.

The truth is, most of what happens in Congress goes unread. Not because people don't care, but because the volume is impossible for a human to keep up with. Important provisions get buried in "technical corrections," and procedural rules are used to hide what's really going on.

I realized that while we were drowning in paper, we had the technology to fix it. So, I built The Capitol Wire.

How it Works

I trained an AI specifically on legislative text. It doesn't just "summarize"‚Äîit acts like a senior legislative analyst. It scans the live feed from the Clerk's office 24/7. When a new bill or rule drops, the bot:

  • Translates Legalese: It turns dense statutory language into plain English.
  • Flags the Tricks: It hunts for "self-executing rules" and waived points of order‚Äîthe stuff leadership uses to sneak things past us.
  • Provides Receipts: This is the most important part. I don't trust AI blindly, and neither should you. That's why every claim comes with a Verification Guide citing the exact page number.

Why it's Free

In Washington, real-time legislative intelligence usually costs $20,000 a year and is only available to K Street lobbyists. That didn't sit right with me.

I built this tool to level the playing field. Whether you're a junior staffer trying to survive your first session, a journalist covering the beat, or just a citizen who wants to know what's actually being voted on‚Äîthis is for you.
