Analyst Brief March 02, 2026

H.R. 4638 — BOWOW Act of 2025

Current Status
Reported with an amendment

Executive Summary

H.R. 4638 amends the Immigration and Nationality Act to deem aliens convicted of harming animals used in law enforcement as inadmissible and deportable. This bill responds to concerns about animal rights and the safety of law enforcement animals. The bill has been reported with an amendment and committed to the Committee of the Whole House on the State of the Union. Passage would signal a strong stance on animal welfare and support for law enforcement.

Key Provisions

  • Amends the Immigration and Nationality Act to include harming law enforcement animals as grounds for inadmissibility.
  • Amends the Immigration and Nationality Act to include harming law enforcement animals as grounds for deportability.

Arguments For

  • Argument 1: Reinforces law and order by protecting law enforcement animals from harm, deterring potential offenders.
  • Argument 2: Appeals to animal welfare advocates, broadening political support and demonstrating compassion.
  • Argument 3: Provides a clear and enforceable legal standard, reducing ambiguity in immigration decisions related to animal cruelty.

Arguments Against

  • Argument 1: May face opposition from groups concerned about the scope of 'harming animals' and potential for overreach.
  • Argument 2: Could be criticized as a symbolic gesture that does not address broader immigration or animal welfare issues.
  • Argument 3: Implementation may be challenging due to difficulties in proving intent and defining 'harming' in specific cases.
