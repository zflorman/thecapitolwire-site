H.R. 6297 — PEACE Act, as amended

Current Status
Amendment in the Nature of a Substitute Offered

Executive Summary

The amendment to H.R. 6297, also known as the "Protecting Europe from Antisemitic Crime and Extremism Act" or the "PEACE Act," focuses on addressing antisemitism in Europe. It mandates consultations and assessments by the Department of State regarding antisemitism and international terrorism in Europe. The amendment requires the Assistant Secretary for European and Eurasian Affairs to assess the threat and the Under Secretary for Political Affairs to engage diplomatically with countries of concern. This action aims to strengthen transatlantic cooperation and protect U.S. citizens and institutions abroad.

📋 Key Provisions

  • Requires the Assistant Secretary for European and Eurasian Affairs to assess the threat of antisemitism and international terrorism in Europe.
  • Mandates the Under Secretary for Political Affairs to engage diplomatically with countries of concern regarding antisemitism.
  • Requires consultation with appropriate congressional committees on these matters.
  • Defines 'appropriate congressional committees' as the Committee on Foreign Affairs of the House of Representatives and the Committee on Foreign Relations of the Senate.
