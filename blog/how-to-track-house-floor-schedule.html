Method 2: Manual Tracking (Time-Intensive)

Official Sources

House Majority Leader's website posts weekly schedules (usually Thursdays) and updates throughout the week. Limitation: No alerts, must refresh manually.

Docs.House.Gov is the official repository for bill text and committee reports from the Clerk of the House. Limitation: No consolidated view, hard to track changes.

How to Do It

  1. Bookmark the Majority Leader's schedule page
  2. Check 2-3 times per day (morning, midday, late afternoon)
  3. Cross-reference with Docs.House.Gov for bill text

Bottom line: Official sources, but time-consuming and easy to miss updates.

Method 3: Congressional Tracking Websites

GovTrack

Tracks bill status across both chambers with email alerts for specific bills. Limitation: Focuses on bill lifecycle, not real-time floor schedule changes. Updates are not instant.

Popvox

Method 4: News Outlets & Newsletters

Punchbowl News, Politico Playbook

Daily and weekly summaries of congressional action with political context and analysis. Limitation: Typically delayed by hours after schedule changes occur. Do not deliver instant updates.

Bottom line: Great for context and analysis, but not instant notifications when things change.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often does the House floor schedule change?

The House floor schedule can change multiple times per day, with new bills added or existing items updated with hours' notice. Committee prints can replace introduced text at the last minute, making real-time tracking essential for Hill staffers and advocates.

What's the fastest way to get House floor alerts?

Automated tools like The Capitol Wire deliver email alerts within minutes of schedule changes, compared to hours of delay with news outlets or the need for constant manual checking of government websites.

Does GovTrack track the House floor schedule in real time?

GovTrack tracks bill lifecycle across both chambers but doesn't focus on real-time floor schedule changes. Updates are not instant, making it better suited for long-term bill tracking rather than immediate floor alerts.

Where can I find the official House floor schedule?

The official House floor schedule is published on the House Majority Leader's website and Docs.House.Gov from the Clerk of the House. However, these require manual checking and don't provide automatic alerts when changes occur.
