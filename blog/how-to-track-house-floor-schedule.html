How to Track the House Floor Schedule in Real Time (2026 Guide)

Last updated: February 2026

Introduction

The House floor schedule changes constantly. Bills get added with hours' notice. Committee prints replace introduced text. Tracking this manually means refreshing Docs.House.Gov, parsing PDFs, and hoping you didn't miss something.

Here's how to stay on top of House floor action without refreshing government websites all day.

Method 1: Manual Tracking (Free, Time-Intensive)

Official Sources

House Majority Leader's website posts weekly schedules (usually Thursdays) and updates throughout the week. Limitation: No alerts, must refresh manually.

Docs.House.Gov is the official repository for bill text and committee reports from the Clerk of the House. Limitation: No consolidated view, hard to track changes.

How to Do It

  1. Bookmark the Majority Leader's schedule page
  2. Check 2-3 times per day (morning, midday, late afternoon)
  3. Cross-reference with Docs.House.Gov for bill text

Bottom line: Free and official, but time-consuming and easy to miss updates.

Method 2: Congressional Tracking Websites (Free)

GovTrack

Tracks bill status across both chambers with free email alerts for specific bills. Limitation: Focuses on bill lifecycle, not real-time floor schedule changes. Updates are not instant.

Popvox

Similar to GovTrack‚Äîgood for tracking specific legislation as it moves through Congress. Limitation: Not designed for real-time floor schedule monitoring. Updates are not instant.

Bottom line: Excellent for long-term bill tracking, but don't deliver instant updates when schedules change.

Method 3: News Outlets & Newsletters

Punchbowl News, Politico Playbook

Daily and weekly summaries of congressional action with political context and analysis. Limitation: Typically delayed by hours after schedule changes occur. Do not deliver instant updates.

Bottom line: Great for context and analysis, but not instant notifications when things change.
