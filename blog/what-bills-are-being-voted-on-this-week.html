How the Weekly Agenda Works

Pursuant to a Rule — Major Bills

These are the headline bills of the week. Each one goes through the House Rules Committee, which sets debate time and amendment rules. Rule bills require a simple majority (218 votes). This is where appropriations bills, reconciliation packages, and major policy overhauls appear.

Suspension of the Rules — Fast-Track Bills

Suspension bills skip the Rules Committee entirely. They require a two-thirds majority, debate is capped at 40 minutes, and no amendments are allowed. Most are non-controversial — post office namings, technical corrections, reauthorizations — but leadership occasionally uses suspension for substantive legislation.

Note: Suspension bills are often added to the schedule with very little advance notice. Sign up for alerts so you don't miss them.

Items That May Be Considered

A catch-all category for items not yet formally scheduled: rules committee resolutions, procedural motions, and bills leadership is considering but hasn't committed to bringing to the floor.

How to Read a Bill on the Floor Schedule
  • Bill number (e.g., H.R. 1958) — identifies the legislation
  • Short title — the common name
  • PDF link — full text as it will be voted on
  • Committee report — the committee's recommendation
  • Rules Committee Print — the version with manager's amendments
  • Added/Updated timestamp — when it appeared or changed
Why Bills Change on the Schedule
  1. Whip count issues: If leadership doesn't have the votes, a bill may be pulled
  2. Procedural complications: Rules Committee delays or amendment disputes
  3. Timing pressure: Must-pass legislation bumps other bills
  4. New developments: Breaking events can accelerate or delay floor action
  5. Manager's amendments: Last-minute changes require updated PDFs

Frequently Asked Questions

What bills are being voted on this week in the House?

The House floor schedule changes throughout the week. Visit The Capitol Wire's live dashboard for a real-time view of all bills currently on the agenda.

How many bills does the House vote on per week?

The House typically votes on 10–30 bills per week when in session. This includes 2–5 major bills under a rule and 8–25 suspension bills. The exact number varies based on legislative priorities.

What is a suspension bill in Congress?

A suspension bill bypasses the normal committee and rules process. It requires a two-thirds majority, limits debate to 40 minutes, and allows no amendments. Typically used for non-controversial legislation.

Where does leadership post the weekly House agenda?

The Majority Leader posts a preliminary schedule at majorityleader.gov, usually on Friday. The House Clerk publishes the detailed schedule with bill PDFs at docs.house.gov/floor/.

Can bills be added to the House floor schedule at the last minute?

Yes. Bills can be added with only hours of notice, especially suspension bills. This is why real-time monitoring tools like The Capitol Wire are valuable — they detect changes the moment they appear.
